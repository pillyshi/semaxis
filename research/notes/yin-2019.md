## Why It Matters For SemAxis

Yin et al. provide the basic NLI formulation underlying SemAxis measurement:
the input text is a premise and a natural-language statement derived from a
label is the hypothesis. SemAxis extends that mechanism by generating multiple
explanatory hypotheses as features rather than scoring only fixed class-label
hypotheses.

This distinction is important in supervised mode. If generated hypotheses
collapse to restatements of the target label, SemAxis approaches direct
zero-shot NLI classification through a more expensive feature-generation
route. Yin et al. therefore establish a necessary baseline for the
label-proxy leakage audit.

## Method

The paper evaluates zero-shot classification for three aspects:

- Topic categorization, with hypotheses such as `This text is about sports.`
- Emotion detection, with hypotheses such as `This text expresses anger.`
- Situation frame detection, with hypotheses such as
  `The people there need shelter.`

It distinguishes label-partially-unseen evaluation, where some target labels
are seen during training, from label-fully-unseen evaluation, where the
classification labels are not used for task-specific training. For the latter,
an entailment model trained on MNLI, FEVER, or RTE is applied directly.

The authors compare simple hypothesis construction variants: inserting the
label word, inserting a WordNet definition, or combining decisions from the
two. This provides an early controlled test of natural-language hypothesis
wording rather than assuming labels are interchangeable with descriptions.

## Hypothesis Wording Sensitivity

Table 7 shows that the choice of label wording matters and interacts with the
entailment training source and target aspect. Definition-only hypotheses
perform poorly overall, while label words and combined word/definition
predictions alternate in strength across individual configurations. For the
ensemble total, the combined variant scores `108.9`, compared with `105.0`
for label words and `49.4` for definitions alone.

For SemAxis, this means readable hypotheses should not be treated as
measurement-equivalent. Any comparison involving generated hypotheses should
retain the actual wording and include sensitivity checks when high-level label
paraphrases drive results.

## Evaluation Perspective

In the label-fully-unseen comparison, the entailment models materially improve
over simple baselines, with the ensemble producing the strongest reported
aggregate total. The paper also notes that abstract situation labels are more
difficult than concrete needs such as water, shelter, or food, indicating that
the NLI approach depends on how readily the hypothesis meaning is recognized.

This is evidence for NLI-based text measurement, not evidence that every
generated SemAxis feature is explanatory or non-circular.

## Comparison With Current SemAxis

| Dimension | Yin et al. | SemAxis Today |
|---|---|---|
| Hypothesis source | Fixed templates derived from target labels | LLM-generated explanatory hypotheses |
| Measurement | Entailment scores over text-label hypotheses | NLI entailment probability columns |
| Output | Zero-shot choice among labels | Feature matrix for a downstream classifier |
| Main concern | Template and entailment-model sensitivity | Label-proxy leakage plus feature variability |

## Actionable Findings

1. Include direct zero-shot NLI using class-label hypotheses as a baseline in
   the label-proxy leakage audit.
2. Record generated hypothesis wording and compare label-paraphrase features
   against more specific explanatory features.
3. Treat hypothesis wording and NLI-backbone sensitivity as evaluation scope,
   rather than creating a separate implementation issue from this paper alone.

## Issue Candidate Impact
