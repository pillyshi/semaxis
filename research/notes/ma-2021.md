## Why It Matters For SemAxis

Ma et al. are a necessary caveat for SemAxis's NLI measurement step. Yin et
al. support the basic move of turning labels into hypotheses and scoring them
with an NLI model; Ma et al. show that this move is not automatically stable,
semantic, or superior to simpler sentence-pair baselines.

SemAxis uses NLI differently from direct zero-shot classification: generated
hypotheses become feature columns for a downstream estimator rather than final
class choices. Even so, the same risks transfer. If NLI scores depend heavily
on shallow lexical patterns, model choice, hypothesis wording, or dataset
genre, then readable SemAxis features are not enough. The score assigned to
each hypothesis must be treated as a measured signal with its own reliability
limits.

## Method

The paper re-examines entailment-based zero-shot text classification in the
label-fully-unseen setting. It extends the datasets used by Yin et al. with
Snips intent detection, AG News, and SST-2, and compares BERT models fine-tuned
on RTE, FEVER, and MNLI.

The key additional baseline is raw BERT using next-sentence prediction style
sentence-pair scoring without NLI fine-tuning. The authors also test the
reversed sentence-pair order and several robust-NLI strategies intended to
reduce shallow heuristic reliance.

## Evaluation

The headline result is instability. Performance varies widely across datasets
and NLI fine-tuning sources, and raw BERT/NSP baselines are surprisingly
competitive. In their main table, the NSP reverse variant outperforms all
NLI-fine-tuned rows on Yahoo, SST-2, and Snips, while FEVER and MNLI do well on
some datasets but not others.

The authors interpret this as evidence that NLI fine-tuning does not reliably
provide the desired task-general semantic reasoning. Instead, results may be
driven by semantic coherence learned during pretraining, dataset-specific
lexical associations, or annotation artifacts in NLI training data.

Robust-NLI interventions are not a simple fix. The tested debiasing or
augmentation methods improve some diagnostic cases but do not consistently
improve zero-shot classification across the evaluated datasets.

## Failure Modes Relevant To SemAxis

1. NLI backbone choice matters. RTE, FEVER, MNLI, and raw BERT variants behave
   differently across classification scenarios.
2. Hypothesis wording and lexical overlap can drive scores. The appendix shows
   examples where small lexical changes in a premise or hypothesis sharply
   change predictions.
3. A strong direct NLI baseline may be hard to interpret. If raw BERT-style
   sentence-pair scoring performs competitively, an NLI featureizer's success
   should not automatically be attributed to entailment reasoning.
4. Generality claims need narrow evaluation scope. The paper argues that
   broad zero-shot text-classification claims are hard to benchmark reliably
   without specifying task families and data conditions.

## Comparison With Current SemAxis

| Dimension | Ma et al. | SemAxis Today |
|---|---|---|
| Use of NLI | Direct zero-shot class selection from label hypotheses | Hypothesis-score feature matrix for downstream estimators |
| Hypotheses | Label templates and WordNet-style descriptions | LLM-generated declarative feature hypotheses |
| Main warning | Instability, lexical-pattern reliance, weak generality | NLI feature values may be unstable or wording-sensitive |
| Evaluation implication | Compare NLI sources and raw sentence-pair baselines | Report NLI backbone sensitivity and feature-score stability |
| Leakage relevance | Label hypotheses define the direct baseline | Label-proxy hypotheses can collapse toward direct NLI classification |

## Actionable Findings

1. In the label-proxy leakage audit, keep direct zero-shot NLI as a baseline but
   do not treat it as a gold-standard semantic oracle.
2. Record the exact generated hypothesis wording and NLI backbone used for each
   run; wording sensitivity is part of the measured behavior.
3. Include NLI-backbone or scoring-form sensitivity in benchmark planning when
   feasible, especially for claims about measurement faithfulness.
4. Treat task-family breadth carefully. Topic, sentiment, toxicity, and intent
   tasks may stress NLI scoring in different ways.

## Issue Candidate Impact

Ma et al. strengthen
[`../ideas/label-proxy-leakage-audit.md`](../ideas/label-proxy-leakage-audit.md)
by clarifying that direct zero-shot NLI is the nearest baseline for
label-paraphrase features, but also an unstable baseline requiring exact
hypothesis wording and model configuration to be reported.

The paper also motivates a future benchmark/evaluation issue for NLI backbone
sensitivity and measurement faithfulness. That scope should remain separate
from the first leakage audit implementation.
