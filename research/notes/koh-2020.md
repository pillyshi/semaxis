## Why It Matters For SemAxis

Koh et al. provide the conceptual frame for models that predict through a
human-interpretable intermediate representation. A concept bottleneck model
first maps input `x` to concepts `c`, then predicts target `y` from those
concepts. This is close to SemAxis's high-level story: generated hypotheses are
measured into a feature matrix, and downstream estimators predict using those
human-readable columns.

SemAxis is not a standard CBM. Koh et al. assume training-time concept
annotations and learn `x -> c` directly. SemAxis instead generates concept-like
hypotheses automatically and measures them with NLI, without human concept
labels. This makes SemAxis easier to apply to arbitrary text datasets, but it
also weakens the usual CBM guarantees: generated hypotheses may be incomplete,
redundant, leaky, or incorrectly measured.

The most important transfer is not the architecture detail, but the evaluation
standard. If SemAxis claims bottleneck-style interpretability, it should care
about concept measurement quality, downstream reliance on the bottleneck, and
whether users can reason with or intervene on the intermediate features.

## Method

The paper studies models of the form `f(g(x))`, where `g` predicts concepts
and `f` predicts the final target from concepts only.

It compares several training schemes:

1. Independent bottleneck: train `g: x -> c` on concept labels and train
   `f: c -> y` independently on true concepts.
2. Sequential bottleneck: train `g: x -> c`, then train `f` on predicted
   concepts `g(x)`.
3. Joint bottleneck: train both parts with a weighted sum of task loss and
   concept loss.
4. Standard model: train directly for `x -> y`, ignoring concepts.

The bottleneck constraint matters because it removes the direct `x -> y` side
channel. If a model uses concepts only as auxiliary features while also seeing
`x`, then editing one concept at test time does not cleanly isolate the model's
reasoning about that concept.

## Evaluation

- OAI knee x-ray grading: predict osteoarthritis severity from x-rays using
  10 clinical concepts such as joint space narrowing and bone spurs.
- CUB bird identification: classify 200 bird species using 112 binary bird
  attributes such as wing color and beak shape.
- Metrics: task error, concept error, intervention effectiveness, and
  robustness to background shifts.

The paper reports that bottleneck models are competitive with standard
end-to-end models on task accuracy while achieving lower concept error than
post-hoc linear probes on standard models. On OAI, sequential and joint
bottlenecks slightly outperform the standard model in task RMSE; on CUB, some
bottleneck variants are slightly worse than the standard model but remain
competitive.

The distinctive result is test-time intervention. Correcting model-predicted
concepts can substantially improve task accuracy. The paper also shows that
intervention effectiveness depends on training scheme and architecture; task
accuracy and average concept accuracy alone do not fully predict whether
interventions will help.

## Implications For SemAxis

Koh et al. sharpen three SemAxis evaluation requirements:

1. **Measurement quality:** a readable feature name is not enough; the model's
   predicted or measured value for that concept must align with the intended
   concept.
2. **Bottleneck reliance:** if downstream prediction can use hidden side
   channels, concept explanations become weaker. SemAxis's explicit feature
   matrix is clean in this respect when the downstream estimator sees only
   SemAxis features.
3. **Intervention and simulatability:** a stronger form of usefulness is not
   just that coefficients are readable, but that editing or understanding
   concept values helps a human reason about the prediction.

The paper also cautions against overclaiming. Standard CBMs require human
concept labels and can report concept accuracy directly. SemAxis currently
lacks gold concept labels for generated hypotheses, so it needs separate audits
for measurement faithfulness, leakage, redundancy, and stability.

## Comparison With Current SemAxis

| Dimension | Koh et al. | SemAxis Today |
|---|---|---|
| Concept source | Human-specified and annotated concepts | LLM-generated natural-language hypotheses |
| Concept measurement | Learned `x -> c` model supervised by concept labels | NLI entailment probability |
| Downstream predictor | `c -> y`, often neural or linear | Any sklearn estimator over feature matrix |
| Bottleneck guarantee | Downstream model uses only predicted concepts | Holds if pipeline passes only SemAxis features |
| Intervention | Test-time concept corrections are central | Not implemented; possible future evaluation |
| Main assumption | Concepts are available at training time | Concepts/hypotheses must be discovered automatically |

## Actionable Findings

1. Position SemAxis as bottleneck-inspired, not as a standard concept
   bottleneck model.
2. In benchmark planning, evaluate not only predictive performance but also
   whether NLI scores faithfully measure the intended hypotheses.
3. Keep raw-text side channels out of downstream interpretability experiments
   when claiming bottleneck behavior.
4. Consider a future user-specified hypothesis mode or intervention demo, where
   a user edits feature values and observes downstream prediction changes.
5. Use this paper to justify separating feature readability from measurement
   correctness and human utility.
