## Why It Matters For SemAxis

Liu et al. show that GPT-3's in-context behavior depends strongly on which
examples are placed in the prompt. Their KATE method retrieves examples that
are semantically close to the current test query and consistently improves over
random example selection on sentiment analysis, table-to-text generation, and
open-domain question answering.

SemAxis uses examples differently. During `fit`, it samples texts to show the
LLM when generating a hypothesis bank, rather than retrieving per-test examples
for prediction. Still, the paper supports two SemAxis design assumptions:

1. example selection is a first-order experimental variable, not a minor prompt
   detail;
2. embedding choice and task alignment can materially change selected examples
   and downstream behavior.

This means `sample_method`, embedding model, selected row ids, and prompt
budget should be recorded in experiment artifacts.

## Method

The paper proposes KATE, kNN-Augmented in-conText Example selection:

1. Encode training inputs and the current test input with a sentence encoder.
2. Retrieve the `k` nearest training examples in embedding space.
3. Concatenate those examples and labels into the prompt.
4. Ask GPT-3 to generate the prediction for the test input.

The authors compare generally pretrained RoBERTa embeddings against encoders
fine-tuned on NLI, STS-B, or task-related data. They also include random
selection, kNN-only prediction, and fine-tuned T5 references.

## Evaluation

- Sentiment transfer: select in-context examples from SST-2 and evaluate on
  IMDB.
- Table-to-text: ToTTo.
- Question answering: Natural Questions, WebQuestions, and TriviaQA.
- GPT-3 API temperature: `0`.
- Example counts: `3` for sentiment, `2` for ToTTo due to context length,
  `64` for NQ/WQ, and `10` for TriviaQA.

The paper reports large improvements over random selection. For IMDB, random
selection scores `87.95 +/- 2.74` accuracy, while KATE with a RoBERTa encoder
scores `91.99` and a task-related SST-2-tuned encoder scores `93.43`.

The retrieval module is complementary to GPT-3 rather than replacing it:
kNN-only with RoBERTa performs poorly on IMDB (`50.20` accuracy), while KATE
with the same retrieval space performs much better.

The paper also finds that fine-tuning the retrieval encoder on a mismatched
task can hurt. NLI or NLI+STS-B tuned encoders are not uniformly better; their
usefulness depends on task match.

## Implications For SemAxis

The main transfer is about experimental control. SemAxis should not compare
sampling strategies without recording exactly which examples were selected and
which embedding model produced the selection. This is especially important
because SemAxis already supports `random`, `kmeans`, and `votek` sample
methods.

The paper does not prove that nearest-neighbor retrieval is the right fit-time
strategy for hypothesis generation. In SemAxis, the goal is to expose the LLM
to a representative or discriminative slice of the training set, not to tailor
examples to one test query. KATE therefore supports embedding-aware selection
in general, but not a direct replacement for current corpus-level sampling.

The task-aligned encoder result is useful. If SemAxis sampling relies on
embeddings, benchmark planning should treat the embedding model as a controlled
factor rather than assuming all sentence encoders are interchangeable.

## Comparison With Current SemAxis

| Dimension | Liu et al. | SemAxis Today |
|---|---|---|
| Selection target | Per-test in-context examples | Fit-time examples for hypothesis generation |
| Selection method | k nearest neighbors in embedding space | Random, K-Means, or Vote-K |
| Objective | Improve GPT-3 prediction/generation for current query | Generate useful hypothesis bank for feature matrix |
| Encoder role | Retrieval quality strongly affects examples | Embedding model affects sample selection |
| Logging need | Retrieved examples determine prompt behavior | Selected examples determine generated hypotheses |

## Actionable Findings

1. Treat `sample_method` as an experimental factor in SemAxis benchmarks.
2. Log selected row ids, embedding model, similarity metric, sample budget, and
   prompt order in run artifacts.
3. Evaluate random selection against embedding-aware strategies, but avoid
   claiming KATE directly validates corpus-level hypothesis-generation
   sampling.
4. Include embedding model sensitivity where feasible; task-aligned encoders
   can help or hurt depending on task match.
5. Use this paper as a lower-level support for adding or evaluating retrieval-
   and coverage-based sample selection methods.

## Issue Candidate Impact

Liu et al. strengthen
[`../ideas/experiment-audit-logging.md`](../ideas/experiment-audit-logging.md):
the selected examples and embedding configuration are part of the result.

They also motivate a future sampling benchmark candidate covering random,
K-Means, Vote-K, nearest-neighbor, and coverage-based selection under fixed
prompt budgets. This should remain separate from semantic deduplication and
label-proxy leakage.
